Decide what a price-probe reply really means.

After a Casagrand group-owner/admin answers the private willingness-to-pay probe, use this scorecard to pick one next action: design-partner call, bounded 7-day pilot, objection follow-up, or park. No public pricing decision is made here.

go

High pain + yes/maybe WTP

Move to a 20-minute design-partner call and define a 7-day pilot metric. This is the strongest validation path.

learn

High pain + no WTP

Do not discount immediately. Ask one objection question: budget, trust, official approval, or unclear ROI.

park

Low pain or vague group

Park politely. The product needs repeated admin pain, not one-off curiosity or free custom automation.

Decision matrix

high pain + weekly/daily cadence + yes WTPBook design-partner call; propose starter monthly after proof.
high pain + maybe WTP + clear owner accessRun 7-day pilot; collect questions handled, RSVPs/interests, admin time saved.
medium pain + maybe/no WTPAsk objection follow-up; only continue if a repeated admin task is named.
500+ member group + yes WTPRequire admin approval path before any live test; do not enter the group casually.
low pain + no WTPPark; add to learning log, no free custom build.

Objection replies

If budget is unclear: Makes sense. I am not trying to sell this now — I am checking whether the admin pain is expensive enough to solve. If the bot saved you 2-3 hours/month and gave a clean summary, would paid continuation still be a no, maybe, or yes? If trust/approval is the blocker: Fair. For the pilot we can avoid private group data: start with 5-10 approved FAQs, one opt-in event/use-case collection, and a daily admin summary. Would that be safe enough for a 7-day test? If ROI is unclear: The test metric can be simple: repeated questions handled, RSVPs/interests collected, and admin time saved. If those numbers are weak, we stop; if they are strong, we discuss continuation.

Do / do not

  • Do use willingness bands as validation evidence, not approved public pricing.
  • Do move only qualified owners to design calls.
  • Do ask one objection follow-up when pain is high but budget is unclear.
  • Do not offer custom free builds for low-pain groups.
  • Do not announce prices publicly until Boogi approves the packaging.
