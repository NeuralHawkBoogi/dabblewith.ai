# Casagrand First City Clubhouse Host Kit

Date: 2026-05-21  
Campaign: Casagrand First City local growth wedge  
Use after: first-10 tester pass or 15+ RSVP signals

## Purpose

Make the first Casagrand clubhouse session easy to run without inventing the flow on the day.

The session should convert local interest into:

- real resident AI use cases
- repeat office-hours participation
- 2–3 community-bot design-partner conversations
- credible proof that dabblewith.ai can activate a WhatsApp-first community

Do not position this as an official Casagrand association initiative unless Boogi has explicit approval. Position it as a resident-led practical AI circle.

## Recommended event title

Use the campaign report to choose the title:

- Career-heavy signals: **AI for Job Search + Interview Prep: Build Your Personal Career Assistant**
- Workflow-heavy signals: **AI by Doing: Automate One Repetitive Work Task**
- Founder/freelancer-heavy signals: **Build Your First AI Sales/Admin Assistant**
- Mixed signals: **AI by Doing: Build Your First Practical AI Workflow**
- Community-bot-heavy signals: **Can We Build a Useful WhatsApp Helper for Local Communities?**

Default title if the signal is still mixed:

> AI by Doing: Build Your First Practical AI Workflow

## Boogi's opening script

```text
Hi everyone — thanks for coming.

This is not an AI theory session and it is not a pitch. The goal is simple: each of us should leave with a clearer way to use AI for one real task.

I’m testing this through dabblewith.ai as a practical builders circle for Casagrand residents. If this works, we’ll keep doing small office-hours sessions where residents bring real problems and we solve a few live.

Today I want to learn three things:
1. What do Casagrand residents actually want AI help with?
2. Can a WhatsApp-first bot collect and organize those needs usefully?
3. Are there community owners or volunteers here who want a simple AI host for their own group?

No hype today. Bring one problem. We’ll turn it into a workflow.
```

## Live demo options

Pick one based on the campaign report.

### Career / interview demo

**Input:** a rough resume bullet or job description.  
**Output:** improved resume bullet, interview question bank, 7-day prep plan.

Prompt:

```text
Act like a practical career coach. Help me improve this for a software/IT role.
1. Rewrite the resume bullet with measurable impact.
2. Give me 5 likely interview questions.
3. Give me a 7-day prep plan.
Keep it concise and specific.

My rough input:
[PASTE]
```

### Workflow automation demo

**Input:** weekly status/reporting task.  
**Output:** checklist, reusable prompt, automation outline.

Prompt:

```text
Turn this repetitive work task into an AI-assisted workflow.
Return:
1. Inputs I need every week
2. Step-by-step workflow
3. Reusable prompt template
4. What should stay human-reviewed
5. A simple success metric

Task:
[PASTE]
```

```text
[PASTE]
```

```text
4. Weekly report metrics for admins

[PASTE]
```

## Community-bot validation ask

Use this near the end, not at the start.

```text
One thing I’m validating is whether groups and communities need a simple WhatsApp AI host — something that welcomes members, answers FAQs, collects requests, summarizes common issues, and gives admins a weekly report.

If you run a WhatsApp group, apartment community, founder circle, tuition group, hobby group, or customer community, I’d love 15 minutes with you after this to understand your workflow. No commitment — I’m looking for design partners.
```

## Design-partner qualification questions

Ask these live or via bot:

1. What community/group do you manage?
2. How many members are in it?
3. What are the top 5 repeated questions or requests?
4. What should the bot never do without human approval?
5. Would a weekly admin report be useful?
6. If this saved you 3–5 hours/month, would you pay for it? If yes, what range feels reasonable?

## Go / no-go after the event

Proceed to weekly office hours if:

- 15+ residents opt in, or
- 10+ high-quality use cases are collected, or
- 2+ community-bot design partners emerge

Do a smaller online follow-up instead if:

- attendance is below 15 but use cases are specific
- most people are interested but timing was wrong

Pause and retest positioning if:

- fewer than 5 people submit real use cases
- attendees treat it as a generic seminar, not a build-along
- no one wants follow-up office hours

## Privacy guardrails

- Do not publish resident names, phone numbers, apartment numbers, or raw bot logs.
- Redact phone numbers except last 4 digits in reports.
- Use anonymized examples in recaps unless explicit permission is given.
- Do not imply official Casagrand/association approval.
- Keep community-bot demos clearly framed as prototypes unless approved by the relevant community owner.
