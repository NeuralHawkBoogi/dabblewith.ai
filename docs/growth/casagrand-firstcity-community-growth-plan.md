# Casagrand First City Growth Plan for dabblewith.ai

Date: 2026-05-20  
Context: Boogi has access to the Casagrand First City WhatsApp community, including an IT opportunities group with ~600 members and a clubhouse suitable for offline events.

## Executive take

Casagrand First City is the best first real-world growth wedge for dabblewith.ai because it combines:

- a trusted local network
- concentrated tech/IT audience
- existing WhatsApp distribution
- physical clubhouse for events
- repeated resident needs that AI can solve visibly
- a founder already inside the community

Do not pitch this as “join my AI startup community.” Pitch it as:

> A practical AI builders circle for Casagrand residents — learn by doing, build small automations, improve careers, and help the community run smarter.

The goal is not just member count. The goal is to create proof that dabblewith.ai can activate a real WhatsApp community, run hybrid events, collect use cases, and convert some participants into future community-bot customers or LexanAI/AI services leads.

## Growth strategy

### Phase 1 — Soft launch in IT opportunities group

Goal: collect first 50–100 interested residents without triggering spam fatigue.

Post one short message from Boogi, not a brand account. It should invite curiosity and feedback, not sell.

Suggested WhatsApp copy:

> Hi everyone — I’m starting a small practical AI builders circle for Casagrand residents.
>
> The idea is simple: no theory, no hype. We meet online/at the clubhouse and build useful AI workflows — job search, interview prep, office automation, small business tools, coding assistants, WhatsApp bots, etc.
>
> I’m testing this through dabblewith.ai. If you’re interested, message this WhatsApp bot and tell it what you want to build or learn:
> https://wa.me/919566112518
>
> If enough people are interested, I’ll host a free intro session at the clubhouse.

Expected CTA behavior:
- people message the dabblewith.ai bot
- bot asks what they want to build/learn
- bot tags intent: career, workflow, coding, business, student, community
- use this data to choose first event theme

### Phase 2 — Poll + waitlist

Run a simple poll inside the group after initial post:

**What would you attend?**
1. AI for job search/interviews
2. AI for office productivity
3. Build your first AI agent/workflow
4. AI for founders/freelancers
5. AI for students/projects

Use poll result to pick the first clubhouse event title.

### Phase 3 — First clubhouse event

Recommended first event:

**AI by Doing: Build Your First Practical AI Workflow**

Format: 90–120 minutes, weekend morning/evening.

Agenda:
- 10 min: what dabblewith.ai is — practical AI, not theory
- 15 min: live demo: turn a messy task into an AI workflow
- 30 min: breakout/use-case collection from residents
- 30 min: build-along: resume helper / meeting summarizer / WhatsApp FAQ bot / lead research assistant
- 15 min: showcase 2–3 resident ideas
- 10 min: invite to WhatsApp community and next build sprint

Important: keep it free for the first event. The conversion target is not ticket revenue; it is community activation.

### Phase 4 — Weekly “AI Office Hours”

After the first event, run lightweight weekly sessions:

- 45-minute online or clubhouse office hours
- residents bring one workflow/problem
- bot collects problems before the session
- best 3 are solved live
- recap posted to WhatsApp and blog

This creates recurring participation without heavy event production.

### Phase 5 — Build visible community utility

Build one Casagrand-specific demo:

**Casagrand Resident Helper Bot prototype**

Possible capabilities:
- answer clubhouse/event FAQs
- collect maintenance/amenity questions
- register residents for AI events
- summarize common resident concerns
- route association questions to humans

Do not position it as official association software unless approved. Position it as a demo/prototype made by residents.

This becomes the clearest proof for “Get a Community Bot.”

## Community-bot product validation from Casagrand

Casagrand is not only a growth channel. It can validate “Get a Community Bot.”

Target prospects inside the network:
- resident association/core committee
- clubs: fitness, kids, sports, arts, book clubs
- IT opportunities group itself
- tuition/coaching providers among residents
- small business owners/freelancers
- apartment communities connected to residents

Validation questions:
- Would group admins want an AI host to answer repetitive questions?
- Would they pay setup + monthly?
- What tasks are most valuable: onboarding, event registration, FAQ, moderation, opportunity matching?
- What trust controls are needed before an AI bot is allowed inside a residents group?

## Risk controls

### Avoid spam perception

- Start with one human post, not repeated promotions.
- Ask group admins before posting if needed.
- Keep CTA useful: free practical AI session.
- Do not harvest numbers from group chat.
- Only message people who opt in by contacting the bot.

### Avoid political / association sensitivity

- Do not mix dabblewith.ai with maintenance disputes or association politics at launch.
- If doing resident-helper prototype, keep it unofficial and opt-in.
- Avoid claiming endorsement from Casagrand or committee unless explicitly approved.

### Avoid overpromising AI

- Say “build small useful workflows,” not “AI will change your life.”
- Event should produce working mini outcomes.
- Keep tooling practical and beginner-friendly.
